\subsection{ALEC Scaling in VMAS}

\paragraph{Experimental Setup} We evaluate post-training adaptation in VMAS Discovery and Transport across three seeds at team sizes $N \in \{2,4,8,12\}$. A backbone is trained first, after which either ALEC or MAPPO is used for phase-2 adaptation under the same budget.

\paragraph{VMAS Discovery shows positive gains for both methods, with ALEC slightly stronger at larger team sizes.} Reward gain over each method's paired frozen checkpoint is positive for both methods at all tested $N$. In VMAS Discovery, ALEC is slightly stronger at $N=4$ ($+0.1484$ vs.\ $+0.0911$), $N=8$ ($+0.1198$ vs.\ $+0.1068$), and $N=12$ ($+0.0885$ vs.\ $+0.0859$), while MAPPO is stronger only at $N=2$ ($+0.1198$ vs.\ $+0.0469$). VMAS Transport is noisier: ALEC is better at $N=2$ ($+0.0803$ vs.\ $+0.0463$), $N=4$ ($+0.4376$ vs.\ $+0.2932$), and $N=12$ ($+0.5993$ vs.\ $+0.4938$), while MAPPO is slightly better at $N=8$ ($+0.4225$ vs.\ $+0.3383$).
