\subsection{Ablations on Memory and Low-rank updates}

\paragraph{Persistent memory helps scalability.} On partial-observation MPE simple\_spread, the memory-only variant is the most stable option as team size grows. It keeps final mean coverage distance low at every tested $N$ ($0.678 \pm 0.059$, $0.695 \pm 0.180$, $0.613 \pm 0.065$ at $N=3,5,8$; $n=6$ each), whereas the memory-free baseline rises to $9.815 \pm 9.203$, $16.325 \pm 12.717$, and $13.425 \pm 12.945$. The SGD-trained memory+communication variant is less stable still, reaching $10.225 \pm 9.575$, $38.275 \pm 16.793$, and $26.127 \pm 23.589$ ($n=6,6,3$). Final rewards show the same pattern: memory-only stays between $-646.90 \pm 31.55$ and $-1200.81 \pm 80.29$, while the baseline and memory+communication variants are both worse and far more variable.

\paragraph{Improved wall-clock behavior shows for ALEC relative to matched RL adaptation.} On a single Apple M3 Pro MacBook Pro (12 CPU cores, 36\,GB unified memory; CPU execution), ALEC is faster overall than matched RL adaptation, though not in every VMAS Transport setting. Across the 27 paired phase-2 runs in the current artifact set, ALEC averaged $3601.0 \pm 654.6$ seconds versus $4493.5 \pm 599.0$ for RL, and ALEC finished faster in 22/27 pairings.

\paragraph{Low-rank state updates appear slightly better and more stable than dense alternatives.} On VMAS Discovery at $N=12$, the standard low-rank $z$ update achieves reward gain $+0.0885 \pm 0.0138$ and improves in 3/3 seeds, compared with $+0.0755 \pm 0.0478$ and 2/3 seeds for dense updates, and $+0.0547 \pm 0.0430$ and 2/3 seeds with $z$ disabled. The low-rank variant is also materially cheaper wall-clock.
