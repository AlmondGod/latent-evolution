\subsection{Meme-level $F$--$R$ alignment (per-run clustering)}
\label{sec:rale-per-seed}

\paragraph{Setup (short).}
After phase-2 training we log $(z_t,z_{t+1},r_t)$ in $25$ deterministic evaluation episodes per seed (3 seeds, Terran, warm-init MAPPO).
We discretize $z$ with $k$-means with $K\in\{8,12,\dots,128\}$ fitted \emph{separately} for each (method, seed) pair, then estimate cluster occupancy~$x_k$, host fitness $F_k=\mathbb{E}[Y\mid s^t{=}k]$, one-step~$Q$, and replication weighting $R$ as in the main text.
Concretely, each agent-step is labeled $s_i^t$ by nearest k-means centroid in~$z_t$; $x_k$ is the fraction of such labels equal to~$k$; the host-fitness target $Y_i^t$ is the sum of the next~$H{=}10$ per-step rewards along the same $(\text{episode},\text{agent})$ trajectory; $Q_{kj}$ is the empirical frequency of $s_i^{t+1}{=}j$ among non-terminal steps with $s_i^t{=}k$; and $R_j{=}\frac{1}{x_j}\sum_k x_k F_k Q_{kj}$.
Subscripts $^{\mathrm{AL}}$ and $^{\mathrm{MP}}$ denote ALEC and warm MAPPO.

\paragraph{Metrics.}
\emph{Weighted Spearman} $\rho_w(F,R)$: rank correlation between vectors $(F_k)$ and $(R_k)$ with weights~$(x_k)$, measuring whether higher-host-fitness clusters also rank higher in replication weighting~$R$.
\emph{Isotonic mismatch} $A_{\mathrm{iso}}=\sum_k x_k\bigl(R_k-\phi(F_k)\bigr)^2$ where~$\phi$ is the isotonic (monotone) regression of~$R$ on~$F$ with weights~$x_k$; $A_{\mathrm{iso}}=0$ iff~$F\mapsto R$ is weakly monotone across kept clusters, and larger values indicate systematic non-monotonicity.
\emph{Shuffle null:} we permute the per-step target~$Y$ across agent-steps, recompute~$F$ and~$R$ with fixed~$(s^t,s^{t+1})$, and average~$\rho_w$ over $50$ shuffles; $\Delta\rho_w\equiv \rho_w-\rho_w^{\mathrm{shuf}}$ is the gap above this structural baseline (tight~$Q$ and geometry can make both numbers large, so~$\Delta\rho_w$ is the more informative alignment signal for comparing methods).

\paragraph{What we see (per-seed).}
In Table~\ref{tab:rale-per-seed}, both methods achieve high~$\rho_w$ across~$K$ and team sizes, but~$\Delta\rho_w$ (above shuffle) more often favours warm MAPPO while~$A_{\mathrm{iso}}$ is typically lower (better) for ALEC at~$N{=}2$ and more mixed for $N{=}4$ and~$8$; at very large~$K$ the partition is an over-segmentation of~$z$ and~$A_{\mathrm{iso}}$ can grow, so~$K$ is a resolution choice, not a unique meme count.
We do not see a clear overall winner on all three of~$\rho_w$,~$\Delta\rho_w$, and~$A_{\mathrm{iso}}$, so we report the tables as evidence that \emph{both} phase-2 adapters yield reward-linked meme statistics under a fair per-run codebook, with differences concentrated in shuffle-corrected rank alignment and small-team isotonicity.

\subsection{Ablation: pooled-seed, per-method clustering}
\label{sec:rale-per-method}

\paragraph{What changes.}
Instead of a fresh $k$-means for every seed, we fit \emph{one} codebook per method by pooling (subsampled) $z$ from all $3$ seeds, then assign every probe. This matches cluster identities across seeds within a method, but can interact badly with differing latent scales between ALEC and a narrow MAPPO cloud if one later pooled across methods (we do \emph{not} do that here).
The columns \textsf{val AL} / \textsf{val MP} are $n_{\mathrm{valid}}/n_{\mathrm{total}}$ seeds for which $\rho_w$ and $A_{\mathrm{iso}}$ are defined (need $\ge3$ kept clusters with sufficient mass).
